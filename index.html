Sci-Fi & Fantasy Girlz

Introduction

The Sci-Fi & Fantasy Girlz is a Goodreads group created on April 5th 2014. The group primarily focuses on science fiction and fantasy written by women. We also discuss works written by men that feature female protagonists/main characters.

This small analysis explores our group's members and the books that we have read and discussed.

The Books We Have Read

To the date I gathered this data, our group had read 24 books from different categories like epic fantasy, science fiction, historical fiction and even chick lit! (according to Goodreads, anyway)

We started doing two bi-monthly selections, then this year we decided to try something different and started selecting one book each month.

Below is a chart that displays the books we have read so far. I wanted to compare how we rated the books as a group against Goodreads average ratings. So in this chart, you will see the group's average rating displayed as green bars, and Goodread's average rating represented by the blue lines.

You can click on any bar on the chart to see more info about the book.

Group Members

At the date the data was gathered, the Sci-Fi & Fantasy Girlz group had 246 members from all over the world!

Our gender distribution is 61% women, 13% men and 26% not disclosed.

The majority of our group members haven't disclosed their age on their profile, but this is the breakdown for those who have set their age public.

A big part of our users registered in 2014, but we have members that have been on Goodreads since 2007.

About this analysis

The data gathering and processing requried for this analysis was done in R using Goodread's publicly available API. It is based on data captured on April, 2016. After processing the final data was exported in json format.

This document is HTML with Kendo Charts for the charts, plot.ly for the world map, and Javascript for connecting everything together.

I will eventually get my scripts ready for public consumption and put them on Github, but for now, no one should be exposed to that mess :)
